VARA Overview & Regulatory Framework

Understanding the Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority and its comprehensive regulatory approach
V

What is VARA and what is its regulatory mandate?

Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA) is Dubai's comprehensive regulator for the virtual assets sector, established to create a robust regulatory framework that promotes innovation while ensuring investor protection and market integrity.

Regulatory Powers:
  • License Virtual Asset Service Providers (VASPs)
  • Enforce compliance with AML/CTF requirements
  • Conduct regulatory examinations and investigations
  • Impose administrative and criminal penalties
Mission & Vision:
  • Foster innovation in virtual assets
  • Protect investors and market participants
  • Maintain financial system stability
  • Position Dubai as global VA hub
VA

What constitutes a Virtual Asset under VARA regulations?

Included Virtual Assets:
  • Cryptocurrencies: Bitcoin, Ethereum, stablecoins
  • Utility Tokens: Platform access and governance tokens
  • Security Tokens: Tokenized securities and investments
  • NFTs: Non-fungible tokens (investment purposes)
  • Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs)
Excluded Assets:
  • Fiat currencies: Traditional government-issued money
  • Commodities: Gold, silver, oil, agricultural products
  • Traditional securities: Conventional stocks and bonds
  • Loyalty points: Closed-loop reward systems
  • Gaming tokens: Purely entertainment-focused
L

Who needs to be licensed as a VASP and what activities require licensing?

Licensed Activity Description Min. Capital
Exchange Services Operating VA trading platforms and exchanges AED 4M
Custody Services Safekeeping and storage of virtual assets AED 2M
Management Services Portfolio management and investment advisory AED 4M
Advisory Services Investment advice and strategic consulting AED 1M

F
Licensing Framework & Requirements

License Categories & Scope:
  • Retail License: Serve UAE resident individuals and entities
  • Qualified Investor License: Serve qualified institutional investors
  • Exempt License: Limited scope activities (specific exemptions)
Ongoing Compliance Requirements:
  • Monthly: Financial returns and capital adequacy reports
  • Quarterly: Risk management and operational metrics
  • Annual: Audited financial statements and compliance certification

E

Which VASPs require external audits and what is the audit scope?

Mandatory External Audits:

  • All VASPs with >AED 50M total assets under management
  • Retail license holders regardless of asset size
  • Exchange operators with >1000 active customers
  • Custody service providers holding third-party assets

Comprehensive Audit Scope:

  • Annual financial statement audits (IFRS compliance)
  • Internal controls assessment (SOX-style)
  • Regulatory compliance attestation
  • Capital adequacy verification
  • AML/CTF program effectiveness review
C

What specific audit procedures must be performed for VA custody and proof of reserves?

Mandatory Audit Procedures:

1. Cryptographic Proof of Control:
  • Witness digital signature creation for all wallet addresses
  • Verify mathematical proof of private key control
  • Test multi-signature wallet access and authorization
2. Balance Reconciliation:
  • Independent blockchain balance confirmations
  • Customer liability reconciliation to general ledger
  • Solvency ratio calculation and verification
3. Segregation & Security Testing:
  • Customer asset segregation verification
  • Cold storage security assessment
  • Key management system penetration testing
