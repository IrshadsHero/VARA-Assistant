VARA Overview & Regulatory Framework

Understanding the Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority and its comprehensive regulatory approach

What is VARA and what is its regulatory mandate? +

The Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA) is the independent regulator for virtual assets in Dubai, established under Dubai Law No. 4 of 2022. VARA has comprehensive authority over all virtual asset activities within Dubai's jurisdiction.

Key Regulatory Powers

  • License and supervise Virtual Asset Service Providers (VASPs)
  • Regulate virtual asset issuance and trading
  • Enforce compliance with AML/CTF requirements
  • Impose penalties and sanctions for violations
  • Coordinate with international regulatory bodies

What constitutes a Virtual Asset under VARA regulations? +

VARA defines Virtual Assets broadly to encompass digital representations of value that can be digitally traded, transferred, or used for payment or investment purposes.

"Virtual Asset" means a digital representation of value that: (a) can be digitally traded, transferred or used for payment or investment purposes; (b) does not include a digital representation of Fiat Currencies, securities or other financial assets that are otherwise regulated under the laws of the UAE or any other jurisdiction
!
Audit Consideration: This broad definition means almost all cryptocurrency, tokens, NFTs, and DeFi protocols fall under VARA jurisdiction. Ensure clients understand the scope during risk assessment.

Comprehensive guide to VASP licensing process, categories, and ongoing obligations

What are the different VASP license categories and their scope? +

VARA issues different categories of VASP licenses based on the scope and nature of activities. Each category has specific requirements and permitted activities.

License Category Permitted Activities Key Requirements Capital Requirement

What are the ongoing licensing conditions and compliance requirements? +

Licensed VASPs must maintain continuous compliance with VARA requirements throughout their license period. Failure to meet conditions can result in license suspension or revocation.

Ongoing License Conditions

  • Capital Maintenance: Maintain minimum capital at all times
  • Governance Standards: Board composition, fit & proper requirements
  • Risk Management: Comprehensive risk management framework
  • Systems & Controls: Adequate technology and internal controls
  • Reporting: Regular submission of required reports
  • External Audit: Annual audit by approved external auditor
  • AML/CTF: Continuous compliance with AML requirements
License Condition 4.2.1: "A VASP must at all times maintain a minimum amount of capital as prescribed for its license category and must notify VARA immediately if its capital falls below the required minimum."

External Audit Requirements

Detailed requirements for external audits, auditor approval, and audit scope

Which VASPs require external audits and what is the audit scope? +

VARA mandates annual external audits for Full VASPs and certain Restricted VASPs. The audit scope is comprehensive, covering financial statements, compliance, and operational aspects.

Mandatory External Audit Requirements

  • Full VASPs: Annual external audit mandatory
  • Restricted VASPs: Required if assets under management exceed AED 50 million
  • Minimal VASPs: Exempt from external audit requirement
Audit Component Scope Requirements VARA Focus Areas
Financial Audit IFRS-compliant financial statements VA valuation, custody verification
Compliance Audit Adherence to VARA regulations License conditions, capital adequacy
Systems Audit IT systems and security controls Cybersecurity, data protection
AML/CTF Audit AML compliance procedures Transaction monitoring, reporting
Rule 5.3.1: "A Full VASP must engage an External Auditor approved by VARA to conduct an annual audit of its financial statements and compliance with this Rulebook."

How does VARA approve external auditors and what are the requirements for Deloitte? +

VARA maintains a list of approved external auditors who meet specific qualification and experience requirements. Deloitte must obtain VARA approval to audit licensed VASPs.

VARA Auditor Approval Criteria

  • Professional Qualification: Chartered accountant or equivalent
  • VA Experience: Demonstrated experience in virtual asset auditing
  • Independence: Must be independent from the VASP being audited
  • Technical Competence: Understanding of blockchain technology and VA risks
  • Regulatory Knowledge: Familiarity with VARA regulations and UAE laws

What specific audit procedures must be performed for VA custody and proof of reserves? +

VARA requires specific audit procedures for verifying VA custody arrangements and proof of reserves. These procedures go beyond traditional audit approaches.

Required Custody Audit Procedures

  • Wallet Verification: Confirm VASP control over all declared wallet addresses
  • Balance Confirmation: Verify on-chain balances at specific point in time
  • Segregation Testing: Confirm customer assets are segregated from VASP assets
  • Key Management: Review private key generation, storage, and access controls
  • Third-Party Custody: Confirm arrangements with external custody providers
  • Proof of Reserves: Independent verification of reserve calculations
Audit Procedure 5.3.4: "The External Auditor must verify that Customer Virtual Assets are held separately from the VASP's own Virtual Assets and that adequate records are maintained of Customer holdings."
!
Technical Requirement: Auditors must use blockchain analytics tools like COINIA to independently verify on-chain data. Manual verification methods are insufficient for VARA compliance.

VARA specifies both base capital requirements and additional capital based on business activities. Capital calculations follow specific rules for what qualifies as eligible capital.

What are the activity-based capital requirements and how are they calculated? +

Beyond base capital, VASPs must maintain additional capital based on their VA activities and exposures. The calculation considers transaction volumes, custody holdings, and operational risks.

Activity Type Capital Factor Calculation Base

VA Custody Services 0.05% Total value of VAs under custody
VA Management Services 0.2% Total assets under management
