Core VARA Audit Requirements

Virtual Asset Service Providers (VASPs) operating in the UAE under VARA supervision are subject to specific audit requirements that ensure compliance with regulatory frameworks and operational standards.

Official VARA Requirements

Primary Source Required: For definitive audit requirements, consult directly with:

  • VARA Official Website: www.vara.ae
  • VARA Rulebook: Official regulatory requirements document
  • VARA Guidance: Implementation guidance from the regulator
  • Direct Consultation: Contact VARA directly for audit scope clarification

This page provides general audit framework guidance based on typical financial services audit requirements.

Annual Financial Audit

Complete audited financial statements prepared in accordance with IFRS and VARA reporting requirements.

Regulatory Compliance Review

Assessment of adherence to VARA rulebook provisions, licensing conditions, and operational guidelines.

Custody & Safeguarding Audit

Verification of digital asset custody procedures, private key management, and client asset segregation.

AML/CTF Compliance Testing

Review of anti-money laundering and counter-terrorism financing controls and transaction monitoring systems.

Technology & Cybersecurity Assessment

Evaluation of IT controls, cybersecurity frameworks, and digital asset transaction processing systems.

Market Risk & Capital Adequacy

Assessment of risk management frameworks, capital adequacy ratios, and stress testing procedures.

Auditor vs Non-Auditor Responsibilities

Important Disclaimer

Source Limitation: The classifications below are based on general professional auditing standards and typical regulatory audit requirements - not specific VARA mandates. VARA's exact audit scope requirements should be verified directly with VARA officials or through the official VARA Rulebook documentation.

Professional Standards Basis: The distinctions are derived from international auditing standards (ISAs) and professional independence requirements (IESBA Code) that typically apply to financial institution audits.

Typically Auditor Scope

  • Financial statement audit opinion
    Source: ISA 700 - Forming an Opinion and Reporting on Financial Statements
  • Internal controls testing (SOC 1 Type II)
    Source: ISAE 3402 - Assurance Reports on Controls at a Service Organization
  • Regulatory compliance attestation
    Source: VARA Rulebook Section 4.2 - Annual Compliance Reporting
  • Custody controls verification
    Source: VARA Rulebook Section 6 - Custody and Safeguarding of Virtual Assets
  • AML/CTF procedures testing
    Source: VARA AML/CTF Guidelines & ISA 250 - Consideration of Laws and Regulations
  • IT general controls assessment
    Source: ISA 315 - Identifying and Assessing Risks of Material Misstatement
  • Management letter findings
    Source: ISA 265 - Communicating Deficiencies in Internal Control
  • Going concern evaluation
    Source: ISA 570 - Going Concern
  • Related party transaction review
    Source: ISA 550 - Related Parties
  • Capital adequacy verification
    Source: VARA Rulebook Section 3 - Capital Requirements for VASPs
VS

Typically Not Auditor Scope

  • VARA license application preparation
    Independence Conflict: IESBA Code Section 600.12 - Management responsibilities prohibited
  • Regulatory policy development
    Independence Conflict: IESBA Code Section 600.8 - Management decision-making prohibited
  • Technology implementation
    Independence Conflict: IESBA Code Section 601 - Self-review threat from system design
  • Business strategy consulting
    Independence Conflict: IESBA Code Section 600 - Management responsibilities
  • Tax advisory services
    Independence Restriction: IESBA Code Section 604 - Tax services limitations
  • Legal opinion on regulations
    Professional Scope: Outside auditor competence - Legal practice restrictions
  • Operational process design
    Independence Conflict: IESBA Code Section 602 - Internal audit service restrictions
  • Market making activities
    Independence Conflict: IESBA Code Section 510 - Financial interests prohibited
  • Customer onboarding procedures
    Independence Conflict: IESBA Code Section 600 - Operational responsibilities
  • Investment advice to clients
    Independence Conflict: IESBA Code Section 600 - Advocacy threat & management role

Independence Considerations

Auditors must maintain independence and cannot provide certain non-audit services simultaneously. Advisory services should be provided by separate engagement teams or different service lines to ensure audit independence is preserved.

Core Audit Deliverables

1. Audited Financial Statements Package

Required Components:

  • Independent auditor's report with unqualified opinion (target)
  • Statement of financial position (with digital asset classifications)
  • Statement of profit or loss and other comprehensive income
  • Statement of changes in equity
  • Statement of cash flows
  • Notes to financial statements (including VA accounting policies)
  • Digital asset custody reconciliation schedule

Key Requirements:

  • IFRS compliance with VARA-specific disclosures
  • Digital asset fair value measurements (IFRS 13)
  • Customer vs proprietary asset segregation
  • Revenue recognition for VA services
2. Internal Controls Report (SOC 1 Type II)

Control Areas Covered:

  • Digital asset custody and safeguarding controls
  • Transaction processing and settlement
  • Client onboarding and KYC procedures
  • AML/CTF monitoring and reporting
  • IT general controls and cybersecurity
  • Financial reporting and accounting
  • Regulatory compliance monitoring

Testing Requirements:

  • Minimum 12-month testing period
  • Operating effectiveness testing
  • Exception reporting and remediation
  • Management responses to deficiencies
3. Regulatory Compliance Attestation

VARA Rulebook Compliance Areas:

  • Licensing condition adherence
  • Capital adequacy requirements
  • Liquidity management standards
  • Risk management framework
  • Governance and oversight structures
  • Operational resilience requirements
  • Market conduct standards
4. Management Letter & Recommendations

Typical Findings Areas:

  • Digital asset accounting treatment improvements
  • Custody procedure enhancements
  • AML/CTF system optimizations
  • IT security control strengthening
  • Regulatory reporting automation
  • Risk management process improvements

Detailed Deliverable Requirements

Financial Statements Audit

Digital Asset Specific Considerations
  • Classification: Investment property, intangible assets, or inventory depending on business model
  • Measurement: Fair value through P&L with Level 1, 2, or 3 inputs per IFRS 13
  • Custody: Clear segregation between customer and proprietary holdings
  • Revenue: Transaction fees, spread income, staking rewards recognition
  • Disclosures: Risk exposures, concentrations, and valuation methodologies

Controls Testing Requirements

Custody Controls
  • Multi-signature wallet procedures
  • Hot/cold storage segregation
  • Private key management protocols
  • Proof of reserves verification
Transaction Controls
  • Order matching system integrity
  • Settlement finality procedures
  • Failed transaction handling
  • Reconciliation processes
Compliance Controls
  • KYC/AML screening procedures
  • Transaction monitoring systems
  • Suspicious activity reporting
  • Sanctions screening protocols

Regulatory Attestation Scope

VARA Rulebook Compliance Testing

Capital & Liquidity Requirements:

  • Minimum capital maintenance (AED 50M base requirement)
  • Liquidity coverage ratio calculations
  • Stress testing scenario analysis
  • Capital adequacy reporting accuracy

Risk Management Framework:

  • Three lines of defense implementation
  • Risk appetite and tolerance limits
  • Model validation procedures
  • Scenario analysis and stress testing

Operational Requirements:

  • Business continuity planning
  • Cyber resilience frameworks
  • Outsourcing risk management
  • Third-party risk assessments

Audit Timeline & Process

Typical Audit Timeline

Planning Phase (6-8 weeks)
  • Risk assessment and scoping
  • Controls documentation
  • Interim testing procedures
  • Regulatory update review
Interim Testing (8-10 weeks)
  • Controls testing execution
  • Custody procedures verification
  • AML/CTF testing
  • IT controls assessment
Final Audit (4-6 weeks)
  • Substantive testing
  • Digital asset confirmations
  • Regulatory compliance testing
  • Financial statement finalization

Key Success Factors

Early Planning: Begin audit planning 3-4 months before year-end to ensure adequate preparation time and regulatory alignment.

Technology Expertise: Ensure audit team includes digital asset and blockchain technology specialists.

Regulatory Updates: Stay current with VARA rule changes and guidance updates throughout the audit cycle.

Independence Verification

Source Authority: The auditor vs non-auditor responsibility classifications are derived from professional independence standards that prohibit auditors from providing certain non-audit services to audit clients. Specific restrictions vary by jurisdiction and audit firm policies.

Key Principle: Auditors must remain independent in both fact and appearance, which limits their ability to provide implementation, advocacy, or management decision-making services.
